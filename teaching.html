  • Intermediate Microeconomic Theory II

    • (Economics 2151B-001, Undergraduate, Summer 2005)

    This course is the second in the microeconomic theory sequence and will focus on the behaviour of decision-makers participating in markets that are not necessarily competitive. Building on the tools developed in Micro I, we first consider cases where firms have market power, or the ability to manipulate market outcomes through oneâ€™s own actions. We will study various imperfectly competitive market structures and show how competition affects consumers, producers and the economy as a whole. This course will introduce new analytical tools in order to study how economic agents can act strategically in order to manipulate market outcomes, which will allow us to have new insights into the effects of economic policy when agents can behave strategically. Finally, this course will discuss competitive markets that are unlikely to achieve efficient resource allocations by introducing the concept of externalities and public goods.
